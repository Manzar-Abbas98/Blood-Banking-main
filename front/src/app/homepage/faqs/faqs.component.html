Frequenty asked questions

Who can donate blood?

Most healthy adults between the ages of 18 and 65 can donate blood. However, certain conditions and medications may affect eligibility. Check with your local blood donation center for specific requirements.

How often can I donate blood?

In general, you can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). However, the frequency may vary depending on the type of donation and your health status. Plasma and platelet donors may be able to donate more frequently.

Can I donate blood if I have a medical condition?

It depends on the condition. Certain medical conditions may disqualify you from donating blood, while others may not. Be sure to inform the staff at the blood donation center about any medical conditions you have.

Can I donate blood if I recently traveled to another country?

Travel to certain countries with a high risk of infectious diseases may affect your eligibility to donate blood. Check with your local blood donation center for guidance based on your travel history.

How long does the blood donation process take?

The entire process, including registration, medical history screening, donation, and refreshments afterward, usually takes about 45 minutes to an hour.
