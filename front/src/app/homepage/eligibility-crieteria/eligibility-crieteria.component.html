Eligibility Criteria

To donate blood, individuals must meet specific criteria as outlined below: Ensuring eligibility involves meeting requirements such as age, weight, vital signs, absence of respiratory diseases, adequate hemoglobin levels, healthy skin condition, and a clean medical history. These criteria ensure the safety and efficacy of blood donations for both donors and recipients alike.

Age Requirement

Be between 18 to 65 years old to donate blood and make a life-saving impact in your community.

Weight

Maintain a healthy weight of at least 50 kg to ensure a safe and successful blood donation process.

Health Status

Ensure you are in good health and free from any infectious diseases at the time of donation.

Hemoglobin Level

Meet the minimum hemoglobin level requirement to ensure the quality and safety of your donated blood.

Travel History

Disclose any recent travel history to areas with prevalent diseases to ensure the safety of blood recipients.

Medication Use

Inform the medical staff about any current medications or medical treatments before donating blood.
